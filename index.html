NON CALCAREOUS RED SOIL – TAMIL NADU

Districts:

Salem, Namakkal, Dharmapuri, Krishnagiri, Perambalur, Ariyalur

(Ref: TNAU Agritech Portal)

Description

Non calcareous red soil is derived from weathering of crystalline rocks such as granite, gneiss, or quartzite. It is red to reddish-brown in colour due to the presence of iron oxides.

These soils are generally slightly to moderately acidic and are well suited for dryland as well as irrigated agriculture in interior regions.

Soil Properties of Non Calcareous Red Soil

  1. Texture: Sandy loam
  2. Drainage: Well-drained
  3. Water-holding capacity: Moderate
  4. Soil reaction (pH): Slightly to moderately acidic (≈5.5–6.5)
  5. Organic matter: Low to medium
  6. Fertility status: Low nitrogen, medium phosphorus, medium to high potassium
  7. Erosion susceptibility: Moderate
  8. Soil colour: Red to reddish-brown
  9. Salinity: Low

Crops Cultivated

  • Salem / Namakkal: Paddy (irrigated), groundnut, sugarcane
  • Dharmapuri: Millets, pulses, groundnut, vegetables
  • Krishnagiri: Ragi, pulses, groundnut, vegetables, tapioca
  • Perambalur: Jackfruit, pulses, groundnut, maize, cotton
  • Ariyalur: Groundnut, sugarcane, maize
